The Two Months Behind A 12-Hour Datathon...

On March 9, 2019, AconTalent hosted Call of Data, the first ever datathon organized by high school students. Over 100 university and high school students from around the GTA created 22 data-driven projects in the theme of Smart City with the help of 20+ mentors and judges, 3 workshops, and 5 speakers with varying backgrounds in data science.

Although it delayed my posting, the past week of vacation in Israel helped me gather my two main takeaways in the two months of event planning.

 

1. Set detailed and transparent fallback plans. As CEO, it is my responsibility to foresee everything that could possibly go wrong and leverage my executive team to draft immediately-executable Plan Bs, or even Plan Cs. How and when to put those plans into action needs to be communicated with the entire team which circumvents last minute surprise or panic.

2. Don’t try to get updates from 10+ people. Just the back-and-forth messaging is draining and my time was honestly better used elsewhere. Even though asking someone to cumulate all information from a department might take an extra hour or so, it’s well worth the wait. 

Thank you so much to each and every guest without whom our datathon would not be possible: keynote speaker -- Terry Hickey, Chief Analytics Officer at CIBC; panelists -- Jean Desgagne, Albert Lai, Betty (Rui) Zhang, Wendy Foster; moderator -- Susie Pan; mentors -- May Masoud, Raj Pathak, Harrison Chow; judges -- Jansen Sullivan, Samin Khan, Wendy Foster, May Masoud, Bella Ainani, Fei Jia; and workshop hosts -- Ashraf Ghonaim, edusphere.io.

Special thanks to Albert lai for his valuable advice regarding people management and the future of innovation. Under his recommendation, I am currently reading Winning by Jack Welch. If you are interested in the essential components of leading a corporation like GE, which is quite applicable in entrepreneurship as well, definitely give it a read.

Event planning is hard work, but seeing everything come together makes all of it so worth it.

10/10 would do it again.
